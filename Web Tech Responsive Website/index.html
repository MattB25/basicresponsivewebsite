History

1890s: Liverpool FC was formed in 1892. Liverpool joined the Second Division in the 1893-94 season. Promoted to the First Division.
1900s: Liverpool won the First Division titles in 1901 and 1906.
1910s: FA Cup final, but lost out to Burnley 1-0.
1920s: Back to back titles were won by the club in 1922 and 1923.
1940s: A 5th First Division trophy in the 1946-47 season.
1950s: Liverpool was relegated from the top flight in 1954. Bill Shankley signed as manager in 1959.
1960s: Promoted back to the First Division in 1962 and won it in 1964. In 1965, they won their first FA Cup trophy. 1966 saw the club win yet another title.
1970s: Liverpool won the League and UEFA Cup in 1973 & 1976 and the FA Cup again in 1974. In 1978 they retained the European Cup and in 1979 the got the League title back.
1980s: Liverpool won the League, League Cup and the European Cup, making them the first English club in history to do a treble.
1990s: Liverpool won the FA Cup. For the rest of the 90s Liverpool did not win another trophy.
2000s: In 2001, Liverpool won the FA Cup, the League Cup and the UEFA Cup, and won another League Cup in 2003. Liverpool won the UEFA Champions League and the FA Cup in 2005 and 2006 respectively.
2010s: Won the FA Cup. Jurgen Klopp took over in 2015, and so far has finished runner-up in 2 cups.

1880s: Everton FC was founded in 1878. The club was a founding member of the Football League in 1888.
1890s: Everton won their first Division Title in 1891.
1900s: They then went on to win the FA Cup in 1906.
1910s: In 1915, Everton won another League Championship.
1920s: Everton won thier third League Championship. In 1929 however, Everton were demoted to the Second Division.
1930s: Everton won the Second Division straight away, came back up to the First Division, and won that too. They won their second FA Cup in 1933. They won their 5th League Title in 1939.
1950s: Everton were relegated for a second time in 1951 and did not earn promotion until 1954. The club have been in the top flight ever since.
1960s: 1962 saw Everton win the First Division again. In 1966 Everton won the FA Cup.
1970s: In 1970, Everton won the League Championship again.
1980s: Won the FA Cup in 1984, two League Championships in 85 & 87, as well as having european success in 1985.
1990s: Despite a slow start to the 90s, Everton managed to clinch the FA Cup trophy in 95.
2000s: In 2009 Everton were runnners up to the FA Cup final.
2010s: 2013 saw Martinez take over as manager, and saw Everton collect their best points tally in 27 years, which was 72 points.

Stadiums

Name: Anfield
Opened: 1884
Location: Liverpool, England
Current Capacity: 54,074
Record Attedance: 61,905 (1952)

Anfield was first the home to Everton FC, but due to a falling out over the rent, Everton moved to Goodison Park and a new team was formed to play at Anfield, called Liverpool FC. The stadium has 4 stands: The Kop, The Main Stand, Centenary Stand and Anfield Road. The ground was turned into an all seated stadium in 1994 which reduced the capacity. The ground has recently undergone an extension to the Main stand which increased it's capacity by 8,250.

Name: Goodison Park
Opened: 1892
Location: Liverpool, England
Current Capacity: 39,572
Record Attedance: 78,299 (1948)

Everton took control of Goodison Park in 1892 after they moved out of Anfield. Goodison Park holds the record for hosting the most top-flight games than any other stadium in England. The stadium has also been the venue for an FA Cup Final and a handful of games in the 1966 World Cup.

Kits and Colours

For pretty much all of Liverpools history, their kit has been red, but when the club was founded, they used a blue and white quartered kit. Liverpool changed into a fully red kit in 1964, when Bill Shankley decided that red would mean danger and power, the kit has been all red ever since. Liverpools away kit has mostly been yellow or white shirts with black shorts, but through their history they have changed to colours such as grey, black and even green. The away kit today is a black shirt with a white collar, with the third kit being a lime green color, with grey on the shoulders. Liverpool were the first English professional club to have a sponsors logo on its shirts, their deal with Carlsberg is the longest lasting agreement in English top flight football.

Today, Evertons kit is a royal blue shirt, white shorts and white socks. However, when Everton first started out, their first home kit was a blue and white striped shirts, white shorts and black socks. They have went through a few colours through the years, with fans and new players disagreeing with choices. Their away kit started as white shirts and black shorts, but as time went on, they changed to amber shirts and royal blue shorts. Evertons away kit today is a black shirt with an orange collar. With their third kit being yellow.
